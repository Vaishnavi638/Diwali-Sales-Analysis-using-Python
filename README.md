# Diwali-Sales-Analysis-using-Python

** Overview **

Festive seasons are a critical period for businesses, with customer purchasing behavior varying significantly across demographics, regions, and product categories. This project focuses on analyzing Diwali sales data using Python to uncover meaningful insights, identify high-value customers, and reveal trends that can support data-driven business decisions.
The analysis demonstrates the application of Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) techniques to real-world sales data, showcasing the ability to transform raw datasets into actionable business insights.

** Objectives ** 

- Identify key customer segments based on demographics such as age, gender, marital status, and occupation.
- Analyze spending patterns and detect high-value customers.
- Determine top-performing regions and product categories.
- Provide data-driven recommendations to optimize sales strategy during festive seasons.

** Tools & Libraries ** 

- Python 3.x
- Pandas – Data cleaning & manipulation
- NumPy – Numerical computations
- Matplotlib & Seaborn – Data visualization
- Jupyter Notebook – Project workflow & reporting
- Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
- Handled missing values and duplicate records.
- Standardized categorical columns (Gender, Age Group, Marital Status, Occupation, Product Category, Region/State).
- Ensured numerical columns (Sales Amount) were correctly formatted for analysis.

** Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) ** 

The EDA focused on visualizing and understanding patterns in the dataset:
- Sales Distribution – Identified mid-range spending as dominant, with a few high-value customers boosting revenue.
- Demographics Analysis – Married women aged 26–35 are the primary buyers.
- Occupation Impact – Customers from IT, Healthcare, and Aviation sectors show higher purchasing behavior.
- Regional Trends – States like UP, Maharashtra, and Karnataka drive the highest sales.
- Product Categories – Food, Clothing, and Electronics are the most purchased items during Diwali.
- Top Customers – Highlighted high-value customers contributing significantly to revenue.
Visualizations include histograms, bar plots, count plots, and heatmaps, making the insights clear and actionable.

** Key Insights ** 

- Majority of customers spend a medium amount (₹5K–₹10K) during Diwali shopping.
- High-value customers significantly boost overall revenue.
- Certain demographics and regions dominate festive purchases.
- Top product categories generate the highest sales, providing targets for marketing and inventory planning.

** Conclusion ** 

This project demonstrates how Python and data visualization can uncover actionable insights from sales data. By identifying key customer segments, spending patterns, and high-performing regions and products, businesses can optimize marketing, sales strategies, and inventory planning for festive periods like Diwali.
This analysis can also serve as a portfolio project for showcasing skills in Python, EDA, data visualization, and business insight generation.
